# 1. ISLAND OVERVIEW

Saint-Martin / Sint Maarten is a 87 km2 (34 sq mi) Caribbean island shared by two nations -- the only island in the world shared between France and the Netherlands. There is NO physical border checkpoint between the two sides; movement is completely free.

- **French side (Saint-Martin):** 53 km2, northern part. Capital: Marigot. Collectivite d'Outre-Mer (COM) of France.
- **Dutch side (Sint Maarten):** 34 km2, southern part. Capital: Philipsburg. Constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands.
- **Nickname:** "The Friendly Island"
- **Population:** ~77,000 total (approx. 35,000 French side, 42,000 Dutch side)
- **37+ beaches** around the island's perimeter
- **Over 300 restaurants** island-wide

# 2. BEACHES

## FRENCH SIDE BEACHES

### Orient Bay (Baie Orientale) -- "The St. Tropez of the Caribbean"
- **Location:** Northeast coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** Nearly 2 km of white sand, turquoise water, the island's most famous and liveliest beach
- **Crowd level:** High -- the most popular beach on the island
- **Facilities:** Full service -- multiple beach clubs (Kontiki, Bikini Beach, Wai Beach), restaurants, bars, water sports operators, restrooms, showers, beach chair & umbrella rental (~EUR20/day)
- **Water sports:** Jet ski, flyboard, parasailing, banana boat, tubing, paddleboard, kayak, beach volleyball & tennis (free courts)
- **Best for:** Everyone -- couples, families, groups, party-goers
- **Clothing optional:** The far southern end (near former Club Orient) is clothing-optional/naturist. The Perch Bar & Grill operates in this section.
- **Operators:** Bikini Watersports, Kontiki Watersports, Fly Addiction Parasail
- **Restaurants on beach:** Kontiki (since 1989, French-Asian, famous red umbrellas), Wai Beach (upscale, design-forward, fresh lobster daily), Bikini Beach (casual, open 9am-9:30pm), Coco Beach, Kakao Beach

### Grand Case Beach (Plage de Grand Case)
- **Location:** Grand Case village, northwest coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** Calm, protected bay with white sand; lined with gourmet restaurants. Features a landing stage. Half hotel-private, half public.
- **Crowd level:** Moderate
- **Facilities:** Beach chairs, restaurants adjacent, kayak and snorkeling gear available, water skiing
- **Best for:** Couples, food lovers (walk from beach to world-class dining), relaxed atmosphere
- **Note:** Grand Case is the "Gourmet Capital of the Caribbean" -- 30+ restaurants within walking distance

### Baie Rouge (Red Bay)
- **Location:** Terres Basses (Lowlands), west coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** Distinctive pinkish-red sand (hence the name). Two sections: east side popular for swimming/sunbathing; west end is clothing-optional. A hidden cave accessible by snorkeling along the eastern shoreline leads to a secluded strip of beach.
- **Crowd level:** Low to moderate
- **Facilities:** La Cigale restaurant nearby, Mezza Luna, Ma Ti Beach bar
- **Best for:** Couples, romance, snorkeling (explore the cave), privacy seekers
- **Tip:** One of the most scenic and romantic beaches on the island

### Happy Bay
- **Location:** Between Friar's Bay and Grand Case, northwest coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** One of the most private and secluded beaches on the island. Soft white sand, calm clear water. Only accessible via a 10-15 minute hiking trail from Friar's Bay.
- **Crowd level:** Very low -- no road access
- **Facilities:** NONE -- no amenities, no restaurants, no chairs. Bring your own water/snacks.
- **Best for:** Couples seeking seclusion, nature lovers, naturists (clothing-optional)
- **Access:** Hike from Friar's Bay parking area, easy trail

### Friar's Bay (Baie des Peres)
- **Location:** Northwest coast, French side, between Marigot and Grand Case
- **Characteristics:** Calm waves, exceptionally clean and soft white sand. Quiet by day, alive at night during full moon parties.
- **Crowd level:** Low to moderate
- **Facilities:** Three restaurants/bars -- Kali's Beach Bar (legendary for BBQ and homemade "Bush Rum"), 978 Beach Lounge (Caribbean fusion), Friar's Bay Beach Cafe (classic French)
- **Best for:** Families (calm waves), couples, party-goers (full moon nights)
- **Special event:** Kali's Full Moon Party -- once monthly, hundreds attend; live reggae, bonfire at midnight lit by Kali himself, dancing until the early hours. One of the island's most iconic experiences.

### Anse Marcel
- **Location:** Northeast coast, French side, in a secluded cove
- **Characteristics:** Nestled between hills providing a serene, protected environment. Tranquil waters, picturesque surroundings.
- **Crowd level:** Low
- **Facilities:** Anse Marcel marina, Caraibes Watersports (jet ski tours), hotel nearby
- **Best for:** Families (very calm water), couples, relaxation, kayaking, jet ski excursions

### Baie Longue (Long Bay)
- **Location:** Terres Basses (Lowlands), west coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** One of the longest beaches on the island. Stretches from La Samanna Hotel to Pointe du Canonnier. Very quiet -- only the sound of gentle waves.
- **Crowd level:** Very low
- **Facilities:** Minimal -- La Samanna resort at one end
- **Best for:** Couples seeking tranquility, long walks, solitude, luxury (La Samanna guests)

### Baie Nettlee (Nettle Bay)
- **Location:** West coast, French side, near Marigot
- **Characteristics:** Long beach on the lagoon side, calmer waters
- **Crowd level:** Moderate
- **Facilities:** Several hotels and resorts, La Cigale restaurant on the lagoon
- **Best for:** Families, kitesurfing (B52 Kiteschool operates here)

### Petites Cayes / Cul-de-Sac
- **Location:** East coast, French side
- **Characteristics:** Scenic bay with boats; departure point for Pinel Island and Tintamarre
- **Crowd level:** Low (it is a departure point more than a swimming beach)
- **Facilities:** Parking (free), boat shuttle to Pinel Island, kayak rental (Caribbean Paddling)
- **Best for:** Starting point for island excursions, kitesurfing (SXM Kiteschool at Pinel Jetty)

### Pinel Island (Ilet Pinel)
- **Location:** Off the east coast of the French side, 10-minute ferry from Cul-de-Sac
- **Characteristics:** Nature reserve island with multiple beaches: main sheltered beach (calm, white sand), plus wilder Atlantic-facing beaches. Outstanding snorkeling -- turtles, conch, multicolored fish at 15-30 ft depth.
- **Crowd level:** Moderate (popular day trip)
- **Facilities:** Two beach restaurants (Karibuni and Yellow Beach), beach chairs, kayak rentals, snorkel gear
- **How to get there:** Ferry from Cul-de-Sac (every 20-30 min, 9:30am-5pm, EUR10 return / USD12 cash), or kayak (~20 min paddle)
- **Best for:** Snorkeling, families, day trip, nature lovers
- **Views:** Hike to the hilltop for panoramic views of Anguilla, St. Barths, and Tintamarre

### Tintamarre Island (Ile Tintamarre)
- **Location:** 3 km east of Pinel Island, French side nature reserve
- **Characteristics:** Uninhabited nature reserve. Excellent snorkeling -- high chance of sea turtles, rays, reef fish. Pristine, untouched.
- **Crowd level:** Very low
- **Facilities:** NONE -- no restaurants, no amenities. Bring water and snacks.
- **How to get there:** Private boat charter, water taxi, or excursion boat only
- **Best for:** Serious snorkelers, nature lovers, photographers, couples seeking true seclusion

## DUTCH SIDE BEACHES

### Maho Beach -- "The Airplane Beach"
- **Location:** West coast, Dutch side, directly at the end of Princess Juliana Airport runway 10
- **Characteristics:** WORLD FAMOUS for extreme proximity to landing aircraft. Jets descend as low as 10-20 meters over beachgoers' heads. The jet blast from departing planes is powerful enough to knock people down.
- **Crowd level:** Very high (one of the most visited spots on the island)
- **Facilities:** Sunset Bar & Grill (iconic beachfront bar with Runway Rum Punch, airline-themed pizzas, pool, beach chair & umbrella rental ~$10/day). Daily handwritten plane schedule on the "Sunset Surfboard."
- **Best times for plane spotting:** 11:30am-4pm (afternoon flights), and sunset
- **Best for:** Plane enthusiasts, unique photo ops, thrill seekers, bucket list travelers
- **Safety warning:** Do NOT stand directly behind departing jets -- the jet blast is extremely dangerous. Signs warn of the hazard. Several injuries have occurred.
- **Live webcam:** Available at SkylineWebcams for checking activity

### Mullet Bay Beach
- **Location:** West coast, Dutch side, 20-minute drive from airport
- **Characteristics:** Quintessential Caribbean beach -- long stretch of soft white sand, turquoise water, adjacent to the island's only 18-hole golf course. The luxury resort was destroyed by Hurricane Luis (1995) and never rebuilt, leaving the beach peaceful and uncrowded.
- **Crowd level:** Low to moderate
- **Facilities:** Snorkel gear rental, some beach vendors
- **Best for:** Surfing (some of the best waves on the island, northerly winds create offshore conditions), body boarding, families, quiet beach days, sunset watching
- **Note:** Popular with both tourists and locals

### Cupecoy Beach
- **Location:** West coast, Dutch side, near the French border
- **Characteristics:** Dramatic sandstone/limestone cliff backdrop -- unlike any other beach on the island. Series of small cove beaches at the base of golden cliffs. Semi-official nude beach on the Dutch side.
- **Crowd level:** Low
- **Facilities:** Minimal
- **Best for:** Couples, photographers (dramatic cliffs), naturists, seclusion seekers

### Great Bay Beach
- **Location:** Philipsburg, Dutch side
- **Characteristics:** Two miles long, the most visited beach on Sint Maarten. Borders the capital city and the AC Wathey cruise port. The Boardwalk (promenade) runs alongside with dozens of restaurants and bars.
- **Crowd level:** Very high (especially on cruise ship days)
- **Facilities:** Full service -- restaurants, bars, water sports, beach chairs, shops along Front Street & Boardwalk
- **Best for:** Cruise ship visitors, shopping combined with beach, people-watching, convenience

### Kim Sha Beach
- **Location:** Simpson Bay, Dutch side, where Pelican and Simpson Bay areas meet
- **Characteristics:** Short but wide and flat beach with fine sand. Protected by a small reef -- very calm surf. Heart of the nightlife and restaurant district.
- **Crowd level:** Moderate
- **Facilities:** Surrounded by large resorts, high-end and casual restaurants, nightlife venues
- **Best for:** Families (calm water), sunset watching, combining beach with nightlife and dining

### Simpson Bay Beach
- **Location:** Along Simpson Bay village and the airport, Dutch side
- **Characteristics:** One of the longest beaches on the island. Former fishing village area -- still tranquil and peaceful. Borders the airport for about a mile.
- **Crowd level:** Low
- **Facilities:** Nearby restaurants and marinas on Airport Road
- **Best for:** Families (gentle swell, safe), quiet beach days, plane watching (from a distance), sunset viewing

### Little Bay Beach
- **Location:** Southwestern coast, Dutch side, near Philipsburg
- **Characteristics:** Protected from open-sea swells by the Fort Amsterdam peninsula. Calm, clear waters with a gently shelving sandy bottom.
- **Crowd level:** Moderate
- **Facilities:** Beachside restaurants and bars, sunbeds, umbrellas, water sports (snorkeling, jet ski, paddleboard, sailing). Divi Little Bay features underwater attractions including a helicopter, submarine, airplane wreck, and "Under SXM" underwater sculpture park (300 sculptures).
- **Best for:** Families, snorkeling (excellent along the rocks), Sea Trek helmet diving

### Dawn Beach
- **Location:** East coast, Dutch side, near Oyster Bay
- **Characteristics:** Beautiful beach near resort area, faces the Atlantic
- **Crowd level:** Low to moderate
- **Facilities:** Westin resort adjacent, casino
- **Best for:** Resort guests, couples, sunrise viewing (east-facing)

### THE LOLOS OF GRAND CASE (Traditional Open-Air BBQ Shacks)

The Grand Case lolos are lined up side-by-side on the waterfront main street, on the beach side of Boulevard de Grand Case. They are an ESSENTIAL Saint-Martin experience -- where locals and tourists eat side by side on plastic chairs with paper plates, eating some of the best BBQ in the Caribbean. No reservations -- first come, first served. Cash preferred.

**General lolo prices:** Main plate with 4-6 sides for EUR8-15 per person. Beer EUR1.50. Sides include: coleslaw, potato salad, pasta salad, garden greens, rice and beans/peas, plantains, johnnycakes.

**Typical lolo menu:** BBQ ribs, grilled chicken, fresh lobster (market price ~EUR25-35), grilled fish (snapper, mahi-mahi), conch, johnnycakes, rice and peas, plantains, coleslaw.

## DRESS CODE GENERAL GUIDELINES
- **Lolos & beach bars:** Casual, swimwear with cover-up acceptable
- **Moderate restaurants:** Smart casual (shorts OK, no swimwear)
- **Fine dining (Le Pressoir, Ocean 82, Le Cottage, etc.):** Smart casual to elegant. Collared shirt recommended for men. No flip-flops.
- **Dutch side restaurants:** Generally slightly more casual than French side fine dining

## RESERVATION TIPS
- **Peak season (Dec-Apr):** Reservations strongly recommended for ALL fine dining, especially Grand Case restaurants. Book 2-5 days ahead.
- **Lolos:** No reservation -- first come, first served
- **Beach restaurants:** Generally no reservation needed for lunch; dinner reservations recommended at Wai Beach, Kontiki, Coco Beach (Fridays)
- **Grand Case fine dining:** Book 3-5 days ahead during Christmas/New Year and February-March
- **Many restaurants close Aug-Oct** (hurricane season / low season). Always confirm before visiting.
- **Currency:** French side uses EUR. Dutch side uses ANG (Netherlands Antillean Guilder) but USD accepted everywhere. Many French side restaurants also accept USD.

## IMPORTANT SEASONAL NOTES
- **High season:** December through April -- all restaurants open, busiest period, reservations essential for fine dining
- **Shoulder season:** May-July and November -- most restaurants open, less crowded
- **Low season:** August-October -- many restaurants close entirely or have reduced hours. Always call ahead.
- **Gastronomy Festival:** Annual Saint-Martin Gastronomy Festival features cooking competitions among the island's best chefs. Check dates locally.

- **Fort Louis** -- Marigot, French side. 18th century. Hilltop ruins with spectacular views. Free. 10-min climb.
- **Fort Amsterdam** -- Heights of Philipsburg, Dutch side. Built 1631 by Dutch settlers. Oldest fort on the island.

### Art Galleries
- **Roland Richardson Gallery** -- 6 Rue de la Republique, Marigot. Historic 1840s home. Richardson is "Father of Caribbean Impressionism." World-renowned.
- **Silk Cotton Grove Art Gallery** -- Marigot. Featuring works of Ruby Bute, cultural icon of St. Martin. Opened 2009.
- **Le Saint-Geran Art Gallery** -- 117 Front Street, Philipsburg. Fine art prints, sculptures, ceramics.
- **Wall of Art** -- Outdoor museum, French side. Created by French artists post-Hurricane Irma. Celebrates resilience.

- **Official:** June 1 - November 30

## TIPPING CUSTOMS

### Dutch Side (Sint Maarten)
- **System:** American-style. Staff rely on tips. Service charge usually NOT included.
- **Restaurants:** 15-20% expected
- **Bars:** $1-2 per drink
- **Taxis:** 10-15%
- **Hotel staff:** $1-2 per bag; $2-5/day housekeeping

### French Side (Saint-Martin)
- **System:** European-style. Service (15%) is often INCLUDED in the price ("Service compris" written on menu/receipt).
- **If service is included:** No additional tip required, but a few euros extra for exceptional service is appreciated
- **If service is NOT included:** Add 15%
- **IMPORTANT TIP:** Ask "Is SERVICE included?" (not "Is the TIP included?" -- waiters may say "no" to the tip question even when 15% service is already in the price, potentially resulting in you paying 30% total)

## CARNIVAL

### Dutch Side Carnival (April-May) -- The Biggest
- **2026 dates:** April 16 - May 4, 2026 (31 days, celebrating 55 years)
- **Highlights:** Grand parades, J'ouvert morning jump-up, calypso competitions, soca concerts, beauty pageants, cultural shows
- **Culmination:** Grand Parade on Carnival Day (last Monday in April)
- **Location:** Mainly in Philipsburg streets
- **Roots:** African, European, and Caribbean traditions. Reflects island's cultural diversity.

## LOCAL FOOD & DRINK SPECIALTIES

### Must-Try Dishes
- **Conch and Dumplings** -- National dish of Dutch Sint Maarten. Pressure-cooked conch with herbs, spices, and fluffy dumplings in savory gravy.
- **Johnny Cakes (Journey Cakes)** -- Deep-fried cornmeal dough pockets. Served sweet or savory. Ubiquitous across the island.
- **Crab and Rice** -- Traditional great dish of Saint Martin.
- **Accras (Cod Fritters)** -- Crispy fried salt cod fritters. Classic French-Creole appetizer.
- **Crabe Farci** -- Stuffed crab, French-Creole style.
- **Lambi (Conch)** -- Prepared multiple ways: stewed, grilled, in fritters.
- **Feroce d'Avocat** -- Avocado with shredded spicy codfish (chiquetaille). Classic French Antillean.
- **Boudin** -- Spicy blood sausage. Found at lolos and Creole restaurants.
- **Blaff** -- Seafood simmered in seasoned soup/broth.
- **BBQ Ribs Saint Martin-style** -- Caramelized ribs at the lolos. Unmissable.
- **Saltfish** -- Prepared various ways. Breakfast staple.
- **Oxtail Stew** -- Rich, slow-cooked. Local favorite.
- **Beef or Fish Patties** -- Handheld pastry pockets. Quick snack.

### Must-Try Drinks
- **Ti' Punch** -- THE classic French Antilles cocktail. Rhum agricole (cane juice rum) + sugarcane syrup + fresh lime squeeze. Often served "build your own" style at the bar. Simple but iconic.
- **Guavaberry Liqueur** -- The legendary folk liqueur of St. Maarten, made from wild guavaberries harvested in the island's central hills, cane sugar, and spices. Centuries-old tradition. Traditionally served at Christmas but available year-round. Visit the Guavaberry Rum Emporium on Front Street, Philipsburg.
- **Rhum Arrange** -- Rum infused with local fruits, spices, vanilla. Sold at Marigot market.
- **Rum Punch** -- Every restaurant and beach bar has their own version.
- **Presidente / Carib Beer** -- Popular local beer choices.

## SAFETY TIPS

### General Safety
- Saint Martin is generally safe for tourists, but exercise normal precautions
- **Lock** car doors, hotel rooms/villas at all times
- **Secure valuables** in hotel/rental safe -- do not leave on beach
- **Avoid** walking alone after dark in remote or poorly lit areas
- **Be aware** of surroundings, especially in crowded tourist areas (pickpocketing)
- **Do not** leave valuables visible in rental cars
- **Petty crime** (thefts, break-ins) is the main concern, particularly on the Dutch side

### Beach & Water Safety
- Watch for **sea urchins**, sharp coral, and jellyfish in shallow water
- Always **wear fins/palmes** when snorkeling
- **Ask locals** about swimming conditions before entering water
- **Strong currents** exist on the Atlantic (east) side -- swim with caution at Dawn Beach, Orient Bay
- **Maho Beach jet blast:** Do NOT stand behind departing aircraft. Jet blast has caused injuries.

### Driving Safety
- Watch for **narrow roads** and steep hills
- **Roundabouts** are common on the French side -- yield to traffic already in the roundabout
- Road conditions can be **poor after heavy rain**
